APPEARS ALMOST EVERY YEAR

1. The 2kg & 4kg Direct Impact (Impulse & Momentum)

"A ball of mass 2 kg moving with a velocity of 3 m/s impinges directly on a ball of mass 4 kg at rest[cite: 22, 36, 64]. After impact, the 2 kg mass ball comes to rest[cite: 23, 37, 65]. Determine the velocity of 4 kg ball after striking and the coefficient of restitution between the two balls[cite: 23, 38, 66]."

Solution Framework:

  1. Conservation of Momentum: m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = m₁v₁ + m₂v₂
  2. Plug in: (2)(3) + (4)(0) = (2)(0) + 4v₂
  3. Solve for v₂: 6 = 4v₂ ➔ v₂ = 1.5 m/s
  4. Coefficient of Restitution (e): e = (v₂ - v₁) / (u₁ - u₂)
  5. Plug in: e = (1.5 - 0) / (3 - 0) ➔ e = 0.5
O'yemi's Hint: This is free marks! Just remember that "at rest" means initial velocity (u₂) is 0, and "comes to rest" means final velocity (v₁) is 0. Don't swap your ones and twos when calculating 'e'!

2. The Inclined Plane (Work, Energy & Friction)

"A body of mass 30 kg is projected up an inclined plane of slope 30° with an initial velocity of 18 m/s[cite: 31, 50, 76]. There is a frictional resistance to motion of 35 N[cite: 31, 50, 77]. Determine: (i) how far the body will travel along the plane, (ii) the percentage of energy lost to friction. If the body falls freely from its maximum position, (iii) what will be its kinetic energy as it touches the ground? [cite: 32, 51, 52, 78, 79]"

Solution Framework:

  1. Find Acceleration: F_net = -(mg sinθ + F_friction). Use F = ma to find 'a' (it will be negative because it's slowing down).
  2. Find Distance (s): Use v² = u² + 2as, where final velocity v = 0 at the peak.
  3. Energy Lost: Initial KE = ½mu². Work done by friction = F_friction × s. Percentage = (Work / Initial KE) × 100.
  4. Final KE on Return: It loses energy going UP and coming DOWN. Final KE = Initial KE - 2(Work done by friction).
O'yemi's Analogy: Think of the block's initial kinetic energy like a bank account. Every meter it slides, friction charges a "tax." It pays the tax going up, and it pays the exact same tax coming back down!
HIGH PROBABILITY

3. Equilibrium of Suspended Mass (Lami's Theorem)

"Two cables are tied together at point C and loaded as shown in Fig... Determine the tension T₁ and T₂ in AC and BC respectively. [cite: 49, 50, 59, 60, 79, 80]" (Usually a 260kg [cite: 46] or 25kg [cite: 12] mass hanging from the center).

Solution Framework:

  1. Find the Angles: Use basic SOH CAH TOA (tanθ = Opp/Adj) using the distances given in the diagram to find the angles the cables make with the vertical.
  2. Total Angles: Find the three total angles between the three forces (T₁, T₂, and Weight). They must add up to 360°!
  3. Apply Lami's Theorem: T₁ / sin(Angle 1) = T₂ / sin(Angle 2) = W / sin(Angle 3).
  4. Solve: Cross-multiply to find T₁ and T₂. Remember W = mass × 9.81!
O'yemi's Hack: The 260kg version of this exact diagram repeated in 2019, 2020, and 2023. In 2024, they changed the mass to 25kg[cite: 12]! DO NOT memorize the final answers; master the SOH CAH TOA steps. Ensure your calculator is in DEGREES!
GUARANTEED TOPIC

4. Perfect vs. Redundant Truss (Statics)

"A simple triangle truss supporting two loads is shown in Fig... Determine (i) whether the structure is perfect or redundant, (ii) the reactions and the forces in each of the members, (iii) state if the forces are tensile or compressive. [cite: 29, 30, 43, 54, 72]"

Solution Framework:

  1. Test for Perfect Truss: Use the formula m = 2j - 3. Count the members (m) and joints (j). If the left side equals the right side, write "The structure is perfect."
  2. Find Reactions: Take moments about the pin support (∑M = 0) to find the roller reaction. Then use ∑Fy = 0 to find the pin's vertical reaction.
  3. Method of Joints: Start at a joint with only 2 unknown members. Use ∑Fx = 0 and ∑Fy = 0 to solve for internal forces.
  4. State Tension/Compression: If your answer is positive (pulling away from joint), it's Tension. If negative (pushing into joint), it's Compression.
