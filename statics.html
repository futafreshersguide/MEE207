Part I: Statics (Equilibrium is Everything)

The Golden Rule of Statics: For any body to be in equilibrium, the sum of all forces must be zero (∑F = 0), and the sum of all moments must be zero (∑M = 0). If it accelerates or spins, you've crossed over into Dynamics!

3.0 Virtual Work

Instead of drawing a million forces, Virtual Work asks: "If I imagine this structure moving by a tiny, microscopic amount, how much work would the active forces do?"

  • 3.1-3.2 Work of a Force and a Couple
  • 3.3-3.4 Principle of Virtual Work & Applications
  • 3.5-3.6 Potential Energy & Equilibrium
  • 3.7 Stability of Equilibrium
O'yemi's Note: The examiner loves testing Virtual Work on toggle mechanisms or interconnected beams. Remember: The total virtual work done by external active forces is zero (δU = 0).

O'yemi's Exam Hack: For Trusses, they almost always ask if it's "Perfect or Redundant". Just write m = 2j - 3 (where m = members, j = joints). If m equals 2j - 3, it's perfect! Then, use the Method of Joints to find the internal forces.
